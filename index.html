COCO
CHANEL

(August 19, 1883 - January 10, 1971)

ABOUT

Gabrielle Bonheur "Coco" Chanel was a French fashion designer and businesswoman. The founder and namesake of the Chanel brand, she was credited in the post-World War I era with popularizing a sporty, casual chic as the feminine standard of style, replacing the "corseted silhouette" that was dominant beforehand. A prolific fashion creator, Chanel extended her influence beyond couture clothing, realizing her design aesthetic in jewellery, handbags, and fragrance. Her signature scent, Chanel No. 5, has become an iconic product, and Chanel herself designed her famed interlocked-CC monogram, which has been in use since the 1920s.

IMPORTANT SYMBOLS/OBJECTS

THE LION

Born in August, the lion held significance to Chanel. She identified with the regal astrological sign, who as king of the jungle, embodied her bold, passionate, and adventurous spirit.

THE PERFUME

From her earliest days there, the number five had potent associations for her. For Chanel, the number five was esteemed as signifying the pure embodiment of a thing, its spirit, its mystic meaning.

THE FLAP BAG

The Chanel Classic Flap Bag is undisputedly one of the most important accessories in the history of fashion. The Mademoiselle Lock gets its name from the fact that Coco Chanel never married.

THE CAMELLIA

The Camellia flower is synonymous wiht Chanel. For Gabrielle Chanel and now CHANEL, it is the beautiful, short-blooming camellia or Chinese rose - a symbol of everlasting love and devotion.

- COCO CHANEL

1883

Gabrielle Chanel was born in Saumur, France in 1883. She was mainly raised by nuns at the convent of Aubazine, where she learned how to sew.

1913

Coco Chanel opened up her first shop in Paris, first designing jersey dresses, collarless jackets, and popularizing the "little black dress".

1921

Chanel chose and debuted the 5th sample from the collection of perfumes created by Earnest Beaux. The scent has become one of the most internationally recognizable in the industry.

1955

In February of 1955 (hence the name), Chanel designed the 2.55 bag. It was the first bag for women to come with a shoulder strap - a detail that offered freedom from the impractical constraints of the clutch.

1971 - Present

At the Hotel Ritz in Paris, Chanel passed away. Karl Lagerfeld took over the fashion house over a decade later, and following his recent passing in 2019, the acting Creative Director is Virginie Viard.
